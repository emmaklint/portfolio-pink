PROJECTS

Simon

The dancing robot rabbit

Simon is a playful companion robot that copies the movements of your gestures and moves his ears accordingly in order to dance with you. He's built using a micro controller, a gesture sensor and a LED light as his nose. The robot is built as a part of a course in physical interaction design with the goal to create a simple interaction that would impact the user's emotions. Simon express happiness when someone interacts with him which puts a smile on the users face. When he gets left alone, he express sadness which really made our users react. I made the product movie for this project which received great feedback.

Physical Interaction Design, Arduino, Emotional design, Prototyping

Enviromind

Visualisation of trade and emission data

Enviromind visualises the relationship between countries environmental and economic data in order to reveal international relationships and provide historical and economical context to the climate debate. I was involved in the concept, prototyping and UX in this project and I made the visual design. This project was one of three nominated projects in the category Best Research Potential at C Awards 2017.

UX design, Prototyping, Visual design, Sketch App, Atomic, D3.js, Information visualisation

Academic Overview

Redesign of an existing LMS

The academic overview at KTH Social is a page where students should get an overview of their studies and see how many credits they’ve achieved as well as finding information regarding their studies. We made a redesign of this part of KTH Social in order to better fulfill the users goals. During the user study we found that lots of students creates their own tools for keeping track of their studies. We used insights from observing their tools to and insights from prototype testing to create a better overview and experience.

User-centered design, Prototyping, Redesign, Information Visualisation, InVision, Sketch App

PapAR

Magazine with augmented reality

PapAR is an augmented reality magazine which uses several different modalities to enhance the users reading experience. The mobile application uses image recognition to display different kinds of graphics and buttons on the screen depending on the content in focus. This project used existing technology and focused heavily on exploring the user experience in augmented reality.

Augumented Reality, Unity, Vuforia, UX Design, User Evaluation
